Early Life & Biography

Darrelle Revis is the nephew of former NFL defensive tackle Sean Gilbert. In spite of his sporting background, his family comes from a background of poverty. Darrelle attended Aliquippa high school in his hometown.

He won Player of the Year of Pittsburgh Tribune newspaper and was featured in the best 22 of the year. So, he was a stalwart from a young age. He also participated in basketball and athletics. In his junior and senior years, he won a basketball championship and also won third place in 4×100 m relays at the state championship.

Career

Darrelle went to the University of Pittsburgh and played for the Panthers. In 2005, in his sophomore year, he earned first-team All-Big East at cornerback. He forwent his final year in college to join the 2007 NFL draft. He started his rookie season with the New York Jets under the guidance of veteran Kerry Rhoades.

Within his rookie season, he established himself as a starter, but in spite of brilliant performances, the defense of the Jets failed to cope up and ranked 28th and 29th in his first two seasons, with teams not testing his side of the field. He was elected to the Pro Bowl in 2009.

He led the Jets to their first AFC Championship game since 1998 but eventually lost. He continued playing a big part in many of the Jets’ games and had some brilliant performances. He was eventually traded off to Tampa Bay Buccaneers. Revis signed a six-year deal worth $96 million.

Darrelle was included in the team for the 5th Pro Bowl and was named SN Comeback Player of the Year. But, he did not fit in the plans and formation of the new coach, and eventually, he had to be released for the first time in his career partly because of his huge $16 million salaries.

Within hours of being released, he signed a one year contract worth $12 million with the New England Patriots. He again advanced to the AFC Championship Game, and this time he won and went to his first-ever Super Bowl. The Patriots won against the Seattle Seahawks.

Despite winning the Super Bowl, the Patriots did not trigger the $20 million option to bring him for a second year. New York Jets signed him for a second spell despite controversies and court cases with the Patriots. The next year, the 2016 season was his worst and marked his downfall. At the end of the season, he was released as a free agent.

He was signed by Kansas City Chiefs for two years with an $11 million contract. He was released after only a year playing only six games. He announced his retirement in 2018, and the Jets held a mini-commemoration ceremony and made a makeshift Revis Island.
