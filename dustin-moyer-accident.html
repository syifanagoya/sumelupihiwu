Dustin Moyer Accident: Know More About This Incident

Dustin Moyer, a 26-year-old from Ruscombmanor Township, passed away in a crash on Thursday night in the same area, according to the police. His loss is felt by all those who had the honor of knowing him during his life. For further details regarding this incident, continue reading this article to gain a deeper understanding of the circumstances.

Who Was Dustin Moyer?

Dustin Moyer was an integral part of Rota-Mill, Inc., where he was employed. Additionally, he served as a Farm Hand at Steven Miller’s farm, showcasing his strong work ethic and dedication. Known for his warm and compassionate nature, Dustin held a special place in the hearts of numerous individuals. His willingness to extend assistance to others at any given time underscored his kind disposition. Dustin’s versatility shone through as he seamlessly took on various roles in his life: a devoted parent, a caring partner, a cherished son, and more. Notably, he was in a relationship with Brooke Manwiller, further highlighting his meaningful connections. His untimely passing has left a void in the lives of many who valued him.

Dustin Moyer Accident: How It Happened

Dustin Moyer lost his life in a tragic incident that occurred on Basket Road at around 12:14 a.m. on a Friday. Berks County Deputy Coroner Todd Kegerise confirmed Moyer’s passing at the accident site in the 500 block of Basket Road. The cause of death was attributed to multiple injuries, and it was deemed an accidental fatality. Fleetwood police, responsible for Ruscombmanor’s safety, reported that Moyer had difficulties controlling his vehicle while driving southward, resulting in the vehicle rolling over. Unfortunately, he wasn’t wearing a seat belt at the time, which led to him being partially thrown from the vehicle and trapped beneath it. The road had to be closed for about three hours to allow the police to conduct their investigation into the incident.
